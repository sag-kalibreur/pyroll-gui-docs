Interstand Tension – Calculation Method

PyRoll GUI  |  Date: May 2026

1. Convention

All tension values are expressed as a signed percentage \(z\):

Sign of zPhysical meaning
z < 0Tension (Zug) – material is being stretched between stands
z = 0No interstand force (perfect volumetric flow match)
z > 0Compression (Staudruck) – material is being pushed/accumulated

The signed convention is used consistently throughout all formulas: tension values are negative, compression values are positive.

2. Standard Solve

The tension value is calculated automatically from the volumetric flow difference between adjacent stands.

2.1 Volumetric Flow

\[ \dot{V}_j = v_j \cdot A_j \]

where \(v_j\) is the exit velocity and \(A_j\) is the exit cross-section area of stand \(j\).

2.2 Tension Percentage (adjacent-stand reference)

The tension after stand \(j\) is calculated relative to the volumetric flow of the immediately following stand \(j+1\):

\[ z_j = \frac{\dot{V}_j - \dot{V}_{j+1}}{\dot{V}_{j+1}} \cdot 100\,\% = \frac{v_j \cdot A_j \;-\; v_{j+1} \cdot A_{j+1}}{v_{j+1} \cdot A_{j+1}} \cdot 100\,\% \]

A negative value means stand \(j\) delivers less material than stand \(j+1\) demands → the strand between them is under tension (Zug). The last stand always receives \(z = \text{–}\) (no downstream stand).

3. Forward Solve / Backward Solve

The tension value is entered manually by the user per stand (tension_after_pct in %). The last stand has no input field ("No next pass"). No calculation is performed – the entered value is used directly.

4. Post-Processing (all three solve methods)

After the tension value \(z_j\) is determined (either calculated or entered), the following corrections are applied as post-processing. They do not change the PyRoll simulation itself.

4.1 Free-Strand Dimensions

Between stand \(j\) and stand \(j+1\), the material travels without tool contact. Height remains essentially constant; the signed tension \(z_j\) changes the width and area proportionally (tension → narrower, compression → wider):

\[ h_{\text{free},j} = h_{\text{out},j} \] \[ b_{\text{free},j} = b_{\text{out},j} \cdot \left(1 + \frac{z_j}{100}\right) \] \[ A_{\text{free},j} = A_{\text{out},j} \cdot \left(1 + \frac{z_j}{100}\right) \]

For tension (\(z_j < 0\)) this yields a smaller free-strand width as expected. For compression (\(z_j > 0\)) the free-strand is slightly wider than the roll gap exit.

4.2 Corrected Roll Force, Torque and Power

Both back tension (Vorzug, from the previous stand) and front tension (Nachzug, this stand's own exit tension) modify the rolling force. The combined correction factor uses the signed values:

\[ k_f = \left(1 + \frac{z_{j-1}}{100}\right) \cdot \left(1 + \frac{z_j}{100}\right) \] \[ F_{\text{corr},j} = F_j \cdot k_f \] \[ M_{\text{corr},j} = M_j \cdot k_f \] \[ P_{\text{corr},j} = n_{\text{rolls}} \cdot M_{\text{corr},j} \cdot \omega_j \]

If only one side has tension/compression (first or last stand), the missing factor defaults to 1. For pure tension (\(z < 0\)) on both sides, \(k_f < 1\) → force and torque are reduced.

5. Ideal RPM (Standard Solve only)

For each stand the ideal roll RPM at zero interstand tension is computed using the last stand's volumetric flow as reference:

\[ \dot{V}_{\text{ref}} = v_{\text{last}} \cdot A_{\text{last}} \] \[ v_{\text{ideal},j} = \frac{\dot{V}_{\text{ref}}}{A_j} \] \[ n_{\text{ideal},j} = \frac{v_{\text{ideal},j}}{2\,\pi\,r_{\text{work},j}} \cdot 60 \]

This value is shown in the results table as "Roll RPM (ideal, zero tension)" and represents the RPM setting that would produce zero interstand tension with the given groove geometry.

6. Finishing Block – Gear Tension

For passes inside a Finishing Block (fixed gear ratio), the actual roll surface velocity is set by the gear ratio and cannot be adjusted individually. The resulting deviation between roll surface velocity and material exit velocity is reported as gear tension:

\[ v_{\text{roll},j} = n_j / 60 \cdot 2\,\pi\,r_{\text{work},j} \] \[ z_{\text{gear},j} = \frac{v_{\text{mat},j} - v_{\text{roll},j}}{v_{\text{mat},j}} \cdot 100\,\% \]

Corrected free-strand dimensions for block passes are computed the same way as in Section 4.1, using \(z_{\text{gear},j}\) instead of the user/simulated tension.

7. Approximation Note

⚠ Approximation: All corrected dimensions, forces, torques and RPM values are computed as a post-processing step after the PyRoll simulation has finished. The modified entry width of a downstream stand caused by interstand tension is not fed back into the PyRoll simulation kernel. Therefore, the simulated exit dimensions (h, b, A) of stands downstream of a tensioned interstand are slightly over-estimated.

This approximation is acceptable for typical interstand tensions up to approximately 5 %. For values above 10 %, a full iterative re-simulation with corrected entry profiles would be required.
