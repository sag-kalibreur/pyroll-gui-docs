Flat Rolling Edge Deformation – Siebel Criterion

PyRoll GUI  |  Date: May 2026

1. Introduction

During flat rolling, the free lateral edges of the workpiece deform in one of two characteristic ways depending on the process geometry:

  • Bulging (Ausbauchung): the edges bow outward, forming a convex (barrel-shaped) lateral profile. This occurs when the contact arc is long relative to the strip width — the material is squeezed strongly in height and forced to spread sideways.
  • Constriction (Einschnürung): the edges cave inward, forming a concave lateral profile. This is typical for wide, thin strips with small reductions, where the lateral free surface is pulled inward by the tensile stress state at the edges.

The Siebel criterion provides a simple, geometry-based rule to predict which regime applies, using only the roll radius, the draft, and the entry dimensions of the workpiece.

2. Geometric Parameters

2.1 Contact Arc Length

The projected contact arc length \(l_D\) (Kontaktbogenlänge) is approximated as:

\[ l_D = \sqrt{R \cdot \Delta h} \]

where \(R\) is the working roll radius and \(\Delta h = h_0 - s\) is the draft (entry height minus roll gap \(s\)).

2.2 Dimensionless Ratios

The diagram is built from two dimensionless ratios, both normalised by the entry height \(h_0\):

\[ \text{x-axis:} \quad \frac{l_D}{h_0} \qquad \text{y-axis:} \quad \frac{b_0}{h_0} \]

Normalising by \(h_0\) (entry height) rather than mean height makes the criterion independent of the reduction level for a given entry geometry.

3. Siebel Boundary Curve

The boundary between the two deformation zones is a rectangular hyperbola in the \((l_D/h_0,\; b_0/h_0)\) plane:

\[ \frac{b_0}{h_0} = \frac{C}{l_D / h_0} \qquad \Longleftrightarrow \qquad l_D \cdot b_0 = C \cdot h_0^2 \]

The constant \(C\) is an empirically determined material/process factor. The default value used in PyRoll GUI is \(C = 0.355\) (adjustable in the diagram controls).

3.1 Decision Criterion

For each flat pass, the deviation from the boundary is calculated as:

\[ \Delta = \frac{b_0}{h_0} - \frac{C}{l_D / h_0} \]
Value of \(\Delta\) Position in diagram Edge deformation mode
\(\Delta > 0\) Above boundary curve ▲ Bulging (Ausbauchung) — wide, flat strips
\(\Delta = 0\) On the curve Neutral — plane lateral edges
\(\Delta < 0\) Below boundary curve ▼ Constriction (Einschnürung) — narrow, thick strips

The magnitude of \(|\Delta|\) indicates how far the operating point deviates from the neutral boundary — larger values indicate a more pronounced edge deformation.

4. Diagram Interpretation

In the PyRoll GUI, the Siebel diagram is displayed in the Results tab for simulation sequences containing at least one flat pass (TwoRollPass with FlatGroove).

  • Each yellow dot represents one flat pass, plotted at its \((l_D/h_0,\; b_0/h_0)\) coordinates.
  • The red boundary curve is the Siebel hyperbola for the selected constant \(C\).
  • The red-shaded region (above) indicates the bulging zone; the blue-shaded region (below) indicates the constriction zone.
  • A dashed vertical line connects each operating point to its neutral position on the curve, giving a visual measure of the distance from the boundary.
  • Each point is labelled with the pass label and the direction (▲ Bulging / ▼ Constriction) together with the numerical \(\Delta\) value.
ℹ The constant \(C\) can be adjusted interactively in the diagram controls. The curve and all classifications update immediately when \(C\) is changed. Press Reset to return to the default value \(C = 0.355\).

6. Notes & Limitations

⚠ Applicability: The Siebel criterion applies exclusively to flat rolling passes (FlatGroove). It is not valid for grooved or shaped passes where the lateral constraint is provided by the groove geometry.
⚠ Approximation: The criterion is purely geometric and does not account for friction conditions, material flow stress, or temperature. The constant \(C\) may need to be adjusted for different materials or process conditions. The result should be regarded as a qualitative tendency indicator, not a quantitative edge shape prediction.
ℹ Literature: Siebel, E.: Grundlagen zur Berechnung des Kraft- und Arbeitsbedarfs beim Schmieden und Walzen, Stahl und Eisen, 1925. The criterion and its extensions are discussed in: Lange, K. (ed.): Umformtechnik, Vol. 2 – Massivumformung, Springer, 1988.
